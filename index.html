What's going on

Each stage plays the same voice recording through a chain of eleven peaking filters centered at the standard audiogram frequencies (125, 250, 500, 750, 1k, 1.5k, 2k, 3k, 4k, 6k, 8k Hz). Each filter's attenuation matches the hearing-loss profile for that ear, so the simulation is stereo: if the left ear has more loss than the right, you'll hear it. A low level of pink noise is mixed in to imitate the noisy environments first responders actually work in.

Real hearing loss is more than spectral attenuation — it also involves temporal processing, recruitment, and cognitive load. This demo captures the loudness and spectral side only. It's meant to communicate the problem, not to substitute for a clinical test.
